# Code of Conduct

CableCheck is a small project run by one maintainer. This document sets the tone I want here, in plain terms. It's the spirit of the Contributor Covenant without the boilerplate.

## What I expect

Be respectful. Disagree about technical things freely and in good faith — that's how the tool gets better — but argue the idea, not the person. Assume the people you're talking to are competent and acting reasonably, even when you think they're wrong.

Give newcomers a hand. Everyone was new to Go, to Linux networking, or to this codebase once. A clear answer to a basic question costs little and pays off.

Credit people's work. If someone's idea, patch, or debugging led to a fix, say so.

## What isn't acceptable

- Harassment of any kind, including unwanted contact and slurs or demeaning comments about who someone is.
- Personal attacks, insults, or trolling.
- Sustained disruption — derailing threads, ignoring maintainer decisions, reopening settled arguments to wear people down.
- Publishing someone's private information (address, real name, contact details) without their clear permission.

## Where this applies

Anywhere the project lives: issues, pull requests, discussions, and commit history. It also applies when you're representing CableCheck in public — for example, posting as a maintainer or contributor, or using an official project account.

## Reporting a problem

If someone's behaviour is a problem, email me privately at **hikarisakamoto@gmail.com**. Tell me what happened, where, and who was involved; links or screenshots help.

I read reports in good faith and keep them confidential. Be realistic about the scale, though: this is a one-person project with no committee and no support desk, so I can't promise a response within any set time. I'll get to it and take it seriously.

## Enforcement

Enforcement is on me, and I aim to keep it proportionate to what happened. In practice that usually means a quiet private word first. If it continues or the behaviour is serious, a public warning; then a temporary ban from project spaces; and, for repeated or severe cases, a permanent one. I'll skip straight to the stronger steps when the situation clearly calls for it.
